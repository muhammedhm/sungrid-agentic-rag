# SunGrid Cooperative — Technical & Installation Guidance

## Site Assessment
Before installation, an approved installer conducts a shading and structural assessment. Roofs with more than 20% annual shading are generally not recommended for standard panel layouts; micro-inverter configurations may be proposed as an alternative in partial-shading cases.

## Inverter Types
SunGrid-approved installations use either string inverters or micro-inverters. Micro-inverters are required for any roof section with mixed orientation (e.g., east/west split roofs) to avoid mismatch losses.

## Interconnection Process
After installation, the installer submits an interconnection application to the local utility on the member's behalf. Utility approval typically takes 10–20 business days depending on jurisdiction. Systems may not be energized until utility approval is received.

## Monitoring & Maintenance
All installations include a monitoring dashboard accessible via the member portal. Members should report inverter fault codes to their installer directly; SunGrid support does not diagnose hardware faults but can confirm whether a system's reported output is consistent with expected generation for its size and location.

## Common Fault Codes
- **E01**: Grid voltage out of range — typically utility-side, not a system fault.
- **E07**: Ground fault detected — requires installer inspection before re-energizing.
- **E12**: Communication loss between inverter and monitoring gateway — often resolved by a gateway reset.

## Roof Suitability
In addition to shading, installers assess roof age and structural load capacity. Roofs with less than 10 years of remaining expected life are generally not approved for installation without a signed waiver, since panel removal and reinstallation for roof replacement is not covered under any SunGrid program.

## Seasonal Performance Expectations
Expected generation varies seasonally; installers provide a month-by-month generation estimate as part of the site assessment. Actual generation more than 20% below the seasonal estimate for two consecutive months should be reported through the monitoring dashboard for review, distinct from single fault-code events which should go directly to the installer.
