# SunGrid Cooperative — Rebate Refund & Billing Adjustment Notice

This document covers what happens when a previously approved rooftop rebate is later reversed due to an eligibility error discovered after disbursement (e.g., an installer later found to be non-approved, or a household later found to exceed the income threshold).

## Reversal Process
When a rebate is reversed, SunGrid issues a billing adjustment to recover the disbursed amount. The adjustment is applied over up to 6 monthly billing cycles rather than as a single lump charge, to reduce hardship on the affected household.

## Which Team Handles This?
Because this scenario touches both rebate eligibility (a determination made under the Incentive & Rebate Programs process) and monthly billing mechanics (governed by standard billing cycle rules), it does not sit cleanly under either the Incentive & Rebate Programs document or the Billing & Account FAQs document alone. In practice, the Incentive Program committee makes the reversal determination, and the Billing team executes the adjustment schedule.

## Member Notification
Affected members are notified in writing at least 30 days before the first adjusted billing cycle and may appeal the reversal determination through the Incentive Program appeals process described in the Incentive & Rebate Programs document — not through the general member grievance procedure.

## Interaction with Autopay
Members enrolled in autopay have adjustment amounts applied automatically to their scheduled charge; members on manual payment must acknowledge the revised amount through the member portal before the next billing cycle, following the same autopay-failure handling process described in the Billing & Account FAQs document for other payment issues.
