# SunGrid Cooperative — Incentive & Rebate Programs

## Rooftop Rebate Program
Household-tier members installing a new rooftop system are eligible for a rebate of $0.40/watt, capped at $4,000 per household, subject to the following eligibility rules:

- System size must be between 3kW and 10kW.
- Household must be within the SunGrid service region (see approved ZIP code list, Appendix A — not included in this excerpt).
- Household adjusted gross income must fall below the regional threshold published annually by the Cooperative Board.
- Systems installed by non-approved installers are not eligible, regardless of other criteria.

Income threshold and system size are hard eligibility requirements checked before any rebate amount is calculated — a household that fails either check receives no rebate, regardless of how favorable other factors are.

## Commercial Incentive Program
Commercial-tier members installing systems above 25kW are eligible for a separate performance-based incentive, paid quarterly based on metered output rather than a one-time rebate. Commercial incentive requests are reviewed by SunGrid's Carbon Markets desk (a separate approval workflow from the Rooftop Rebate Program).

## Appeals Process
Members disputing a rebate eligibility determination may appeal within 30 days of the determination. Appeals are reviewed by the Incentive Program committee, separate from the general member grievance procedure described in the Program Policies document.

## Program Funding Status
The Rooftop Rebate Program is funded on a rolling annual budget. When the annual budget is exhausted, new applications are placed on a waitlist until the following fiscal year's budget is released.

## Interaction with Other Incentive Programs
A household participating in the Rooftop Rebate Program may also apply separately for the Battery Storage Add-On Incentive, provided the rooftop system has already received a favorable eligibility determination. Households may not apply for the Battery Storage Add-On Incentive independently of an approved Rooftop Rebate Program application. The Low-Income Bill Credit Program, by contrast, has no dependency on Rooftop Rebate status and may be applied for independently.

## Documentation Requirements
Applicants must submit proof of installer approval status, a copy of the interconnection application filed with the local utility, and a signed income attestation. Incomplete applications are held in a pending queue for up to 15 business days before being automatically closed; applicants must reapply after closure.
